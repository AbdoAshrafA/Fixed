Types

Types of Impression Errors

Voids

The complete tooth anatomy and/or gumline was not fully captured by the impression material around it

Bubbles

This is just what it sounds like, it’s an air bubble in the impression

Thin walls

This is when the material itself captures the majority of the tooth anatomy, however the scanning process does not pick up the tray well

Double imprints

This is when you see two gumlines and/or what looks like two teeth imprints one right next to or on top of the other


Pulls

A “pulled” effect around the gingival margin of the impression


Tears

When the impression material has torn in some way, breaking out of place and undermining the accuracy of the impression


Insufficient material

Not enough material was used

No light body

No light body was used. The light body and heavy body are usually two different colors, so that they can be identified easily

Causes

Causes of Impression Errors

  • Voids
    Causes
    1. Insufficient material in the impression tray
  • Bubbles
    Causes
    1. Mixing tip being lifted out of the material mid process, creating a hollow void under the material now placed on top

    2. Excessive saliva in the patient's mouth
  • Thin walls
    Causes
    1. Insufficient material, too small trays or the tray was pushed in too deep
  • Double imprints
    Causes
    1. The tray, full arch or quadrant is moved during the setting process

    2. The first-step is not done correctly
  • Pulls
    Causes
    1. The impression material prematurely sets before insertion and results in a “pulled” effect around the gingival margin

    2. When the impression material has not fully set and is removed (pulled) from the mouth too soon
  • Tears
    Causes
    1. When the patient has extreme undercuts or black triangles which causes the impression material to lock into the interproximal and tear when removing the impression

    2. The material has not fully set before removal
  • Insufficient material
    Causes
    1. Inadequate measurements of material for the size impression tray used
  • No light body
    Causes
    1. No light body was used

Prevention

How to prevent Impression Errors?

Preparation Steps

1. Educate the patient on the importance of keeping their teeth still during the impression process

2. Select an appropriate tray and test fit it in the patient’s mouth to ensure it extends beyond the most distal tooth

3. Use wax to block out undercuts before taking the impression, if necessary

4. Remove any excess saliva or debris from the patient’s mouth

Material Handling

1. Make sure you’re using enough material—too much is better than too little

2. Keep the mixing tip inserted in the material while dispensing to avoid introducing air bubbles.

Taking the Impression

1. Once the material is ready, put the tray in the patient’s mouth as soon as possible to prevent premature setting

2. Gently seat the tray into the teeth without applying excessive pressure, ensuring even distribution of the material

3. Use a plastic sheet for medium/heavy body impressions during the two-step technique to create a rough initial impression

Final Steps

1. Leave the tray in the patient’s mouth 60–90 seconds longer than the provided instructions to ensure complete setting

2. Remove the tray carefully to avoid tears or distortion in the impression material
